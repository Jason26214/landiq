# Australian Land Due Diligence & Feasibility Analysis

## 1. Step-by-Step Due Diligence Process

### Phase 1: Site Identification & Initial Screening

**Step 1 — Opportunity Sourcing**
- Sites sourced from: commercial agents, off-market networks, council land release databases, CoreLogic searches, direct landowner approaches
- Developers maintain acquisition briefs: target LGAs, minimum lot sizes, zoning, price range

**Step 2 — Desktop Screening (1–2 days)**
- Pull title search from state Land Registry
- Check current zoning (LEP / planning scheme)
- Review lot size, shape, frontage, orientation via cadastral maps
- Check constraints: flood, bushfire, heritage, contamination, native title
- Quick "back of envelope" feasibility

**Step 3 — Preliminary Feasibility (1–3 days)**
- Estimate dwelling yield / GFA from zoning controls (FSR, height, minimum lot size)
- Estimate Gross Realisable Value (GRV) from comparable sales
- Estimate construction costs (per-sqm benchmarks)
- Calculate preliminary development margin
- Target: 15–20% margin on cost for residential. Below threshold = abandon

### Phase 2: Detailed Due Diligence (Under Contract / Exclusivity)

Typically enter conditional contract (subject to DD, planning, finance) or obtain option/exclusivity.

**Step 4 — Title & Legal Review**
- Certificate of Title, registered instruments
- Easements, restrictive covenants, caveats, encumbrances
- s10.7 planning certificates (NSW) or equivalent
- Compulsory acquisition notices, road widening proposals

**Step 5 — Planning & Zoning Analysis**
- **LEP**: zoning, FSR, height, minimum lot size, heritage, flooding
- **DCP**: setbacks, parking, landscaping, design, acoustic requirements
- **SEPP**: state-level instruments (housing, resilience, transport)
- Maximum development envelope (footprint, height, setbacks)
- Permissibility: with consent / complying / prohibited
- Council strategic direction: proposed rezonings or upzonings
- Pre-DA meeting with council

**Step 6 — Site Investigations**
| Investigation | Consultant | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Detail/Feature Survey | Registered Surveyor | Levels, contours, trees, structures, boundaries, services |
| Geotechnical | Geotech Engineer | Soil bearing, rock, groundwater, reactivity, excavation |
| Contamination (Phase 1 PSI) | Environmental Consultant | Historical review, aerial photos, EPA registers, site walkover |
| Contamination (Phase 2 DSI) | Environmental Consultant | Soil/groundwater sampling (if Phase 1 flags issues) |
| Ecological/Biodiversity | Ecologist | Threatened species, vegetation, offsets |
| Bushfire (BAL) | Bushfire Consultant | BAL assessment, APZ requirements |
| Flood Assessment | Hydraulic Engineer | Flood levels, site-specific modelling |
| Heritage | Heritage Consultant | Aboriginal (AHIMS) and European heritage |
| Traffic & Access | Traffic Engineer | Access feasibility, intersection capacity, road upgrades |
| Services/Infrastructure | Various utilities | Water, sewer, electricity, gas, telecom capacity |
| Acoustic | Acoustic Consultant | Noise impacts, construction requirements |
| Arborist Report | Arborist | Tree health, significance, TPZ constraints |

**Step 7 — Refined Feasibility Analysis**
- Update feasibility model with all consultant findings
- Adjust yield (typically downward due to constraints)
- Obtain accurate construction costs (QS or builder pricing)
- Factor in: infrastructure contributions (s7.11/7.12, etc.), consultant/application/holding costs
- Model sensitivity scenarios (price drops, cost overruns, delays)
- Key metrics: development margin, ROE, IRR, residual land value

### Phase 3: Acquisition Decision

**Step 8 — Risk Assessment & Go/No-Go**
- Review feasibility, consultant reports, risk register
- Key factors: margin, planning risk, market, funding, timeline, competition
- Proceed → satisfy contract conditions → settle
- Abandon → exercise DD termination clause → walk away

**Step 9 — Post-Acquisition / Pre-DA**
- Engage architects for scheme design and DA documentation
- Lodge Development Application
- Continue council/stakeholder engagement

## 2. Feasibility Report Structure

### A. Project Summary
- Site address, lot/DP, LGA, zoning
- Proposed development type
- Key financials: total cost, GRV, profit, margin

### B. Revenue / Gross Realisable Value (GRV)
- Product mix (lots, apartments, townhouses by bedroom)
- Sale prices per product (with comparable sales evidence)
- Total GRV (before/after GST)
- Presale assumptions

### C. Land Cost
- Purchase price, stamp duty, legal costs
- Holding costs (rates, land tax, insurance, interest)

### D. Hard Construction Costs
- Site preparation (demolition, excavation, remediation)
- Civil/infrastructure works
- Building construction (per sqm GFA)
- Contingency (5–10%)

### E. Soft Costs / Professional Fees
- Architect, town planner, surveyor, geotech, environmental, traffic, landscape, acoustic, arborist, QS, project manager, legal, sales & marketing

### F. Statutory Costs
- DA fees, long service levy, CC/building permit fees
- s7.11/7.12 contributions, headworks charges, electricity supply charges

### G. Finance Costs
- Land finance, construction finance (drawdown profile, interest, fees)
- Mezzanine/equity assumptions

### H. Project Cashflow / Timeline
- Month-by-month cashflow
- Key milestones: acquisition → DA → CC → construction → completion → settlement

### I. Key Financial Metrics
| Metric | Formula | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Total Development Cost (TDC) | Land + construction + soft + statutory + finance | — |
| Gross Realisable Value (GRV) | Sum of all sale prices | — |
| Development Profit | GRV - TDC | — |
| Development Margin (on cost) | Profit / TDC × 100 | 15–20% residential |
| Margin on GRV | Profit / GRV × 100 | 12–18% |
| Residual Land Value (RLV) | GRV - all costs except land - target profit | Max land price |
| Return on Equity (ROE) | Profit / equity × 100 | 25–40%+ |
| Internal Rate of Return (IRR) | Discount rate where NPV = 0 | 20–30%+ |
| Peak Debt | Max cumulative borrowing | — |

### J. Sensitivity Analysis
- Price variations (+/- 5%, 10%)
- Cost variations (+/- 5%, 10%)
- Time delays (3, 6, 12 months)
- Interest rate changes
- Presented as matrix tables

### K. Risk Register
- Key risks with likelihood, consequence, mitigation

## 3. Key Planning & Design Metrics

| Metric | Definition |
|---|---|
| **FSR / FAR** | Total GFA / site area. Controls building bulk (e.g., FSR 2:1 on 1,000sqm = 2,000sqm GFA) |
| **Height of Building (HOB)** | Maximum height in metres and/or storeys |
| **Site Coverage** | Building footprint / site area × 100. Typically 40–60% residential |
| **Setbacks** | Minimum distances from boundaries (front, side, rear) |
| **Dwelling Yield** | Number achievable. Subdivision: site / min lot size (minus ~30% roads/open space). Apartments: GFA / avg apartment size |
| **Open Space** | Required percentage (often 10–15% for larger subdivisions) |
| **Car Parking Rate** | Spaces per dwelling (e.g., 1 per 1-bed, 2 per 3-bed + visitor) |
| **Landscaped Area** | Minimum deep soil / landscaped area (15–30% of site) |
| **BASIX (NSW)** | Energy, water, thermal comfort targets |
| **NatHERS** | Nationwide energy rating — minimum 6–7 stars for new dwellings |

### Quick Yield Estimation

**Subdivision:**
- Net developable = gross area - roads/infrastructure (~25–35%)
- Lot yield = net developable / average lot size

**Apartments:**
- Max GFA = site area × FSR
- Saleable area = GFA × efficiency (75–85%)
- Dwellings = saleable area / avg apartment size (~75sqm)

**Townhouses:**
- Often: minimum lot width (6m) × frontage, or site area / min lot size per dwelling

## 4. Australian Planning System Overview

### Three-tier system: Federal → State → Local

Land use planning is primarily **state and local government** responsibility.

### NSW
- **EP&A Act 1979** — primary legislation
- **LEP** (Local Environmental Plan): statutory, zoning, FSR, height, heritage, flood, min lot size
- **DCP** (Development Control Plan): non-statutory guidelines, detailed design controls
- **SEPP** (State Environmental Planning Policy): state-level, overrides/supplements LEPs
- **s10.7 Certificate**: authoritative summary of all planning controls on a parcel

### Victoria
- **Planning and Environment Act 1987**
- **Planning Scheme** per municipality (combines LEP + DCP equivalent)
- Zones + Overlays + Particular Provisions
- **ResCode** (Clause 54/55/56): residential design standards

### Queensland
- **Planning Act 2016**
- **Planning Scheme** per council: zones, overlays, codes
- **SPP** (State Planning Policy): state interests
- Assessment categories: Accepted → Code assessable → Impact assessable

### SA
- **Planning and Design Code** (single state-wide code, replaced council plans 2021)

### WA
- **Local Planning Scheme** per council
- **R-Codes**: state-wide residential design standards (density, setbacks)

### Key Concepts (All States)
- Zoning = permitted/prohibited uses
- Overlays = site-specific constraints (heritage, flood, bushfire, environment)
- Development contributions = mandatory infrastructure charges
- Referral authorities = state agencies (transport, environment, water, fire)
- Appeals = planning tribunal/court (NSW: L&E Court, VIC: VCAT, QLD: P&E Court)

## 5. Common Risks & Red Flags

### Environmental & Physical
- **Flooding**: hydraulic studies needed, floor level raising, can sterilise site portions
- **Bushfire**: BAL assessment, APZs (10–100m setbacks), AS 3959 construction
- **Contamination**: former industrial/petrol station/landfill. Remediation $500k–$10M+
- **Acid Sulfate Soils**: common in coastal/low-lying areas
- **Geotechnical**: rock (expensive excavation), high water table, reactive soils
- **Ecological**: threatened species, endangered communities, riparian corridors, offsets

### Legal & Title
- **Easements**: can sterilise buildable area
- **Restrictive covenants**: may limit buildings, uses, materials
- **Native title**: affects greenfield/broadacre sites
- **Encroachments**: neighbouring structures crossing boundary

### Planning & Approval
- **Neighbour objections**: infill/apartments attract opposition → refusal or conditions
- **Heritage**: design requirements, Heritage Council referral
- **Council attitude**: some notoriously slow/hostile
- **Changing controls**: draft amendments during assessment
- **State infrastructure requirements**: road upgrades, land dedication

### Market & Financial
- **Market downturn**: price falls between acquisition and sale
- **Construction cost escalation**: 20–30% increases in recent cycles
- **Interest rate risk**: higher holding costs, reduced buyer capacity
- **Presale risk**: lenders require 80–100% debt coverage from presales
- **Settlement risk**: buyers failing to settle off-the-plan

## 8. Industry Pain Points & Automation Opportunities

### Top Pain Points
1. **Manual data gathering** — 4–8 hours per site across dozens of government websites
2. **Fragmented planning data** — LEPs, DCPs, council websites, state portals, verbal advice
3. **Error-prone Excel feasibility** — fragile, not connected to planning data, poor version control
4. **Yield estimation needs specialist knowledge** — FSR × height × setbacks → realistic dwelling count
5. **Chaotic consultant coordination** — 8–15 consultants managed via email and spreadsheets
6. **Slow/unpredictable council processes** — DA times: 3 months to 2+ years
7. **Late discovery of fatal flaws** — contamination, heritage found deep into DD
8. **Difficult comparable sales assembly** — multiple platforms, manual analysis
9. **Tedious sensitivity analysis** — manual spreadsheet manipulation
10. **Knowledge trapped in people's heads** — council attitudes, local conditions

### Highest-Value Automation Opportunities

| Opportunity | Pain | Feasibility |
|---|---|---|
| Automated planning controls extraction | Very High | High |
| Constraint screening dashboard | Very High | High |
| Quick yield estimation | Very High | Medium |
| Integrated feasibility engine | Very High | Medium-High |
| Comparable sales analysis | High | Medium |
| Consultant workflow management | High | High |
| Dynamic due diligence checklist | High | High |
| Report generation | Medium-High | High |
| Council intelligence database | Medium-High | Medium |
| Instant RLV calculator | High | High |
