## Speaker B — Slides 6–10

### Slide 6 — How It Works (~40s)

Thanks. LandIQ works in three simple steps.

Step one — you enter any Australian property address or lot number.

Step two — our AI queries over thirty government data sources in real time. We're pulling zoning, floor space ratio, height limits, bushfire risk, flood risk, heritage overlays, and more.

Step three — you get a complete feasibility report. AI-generated analysis with risk scoring and development recommendations, available as PDF, Word, or an interactive dashboard.

### Slide 7 — Data Engine (~50s)

Let's talk about the data engine — this is the backbone of LandIQ.

We query NSW ePlanning via ArcGIS REST and VIC OpenData via WFS endpoints, in parallel. That means comprehensive planning controls for any parcel delivered in under three hundred milliseconds.

As you can see in the table, we cover zoning, FSR, height limits, bushfire, flood, heritage, cadastre, and easements across both states. Response times range from about a hundred and thirty to two hundred and ten milliseconds.

And here's the key part — all of these endpoints are free and open. No API key required. This is public government data that we're making accessible and actionable.

### Slide 8 — AI Report (~40s)

Once we have the data, our AI report engine turns it into an institutional-grade feasibility report in minutes.

The report covers five core sections: site overview, planning analysis, development potential, risk assessment, and recommendations. Each section is generated by AI, and the full report can be exported as PDF or Word.

Under the hood, we're parsing council DCP PDFs through our LLM, automatically detecting flood, bushfire, heritage and contamination risks, calculating GFA, dwelling yield and density, and running comparable sales analysis.

### Slide 9 — Demo (~45s)

This is our interactive map interface, built with Leaflet and CartoDB tiles. Unlike a regular Google Map, this is a polygon-based map — it shows actual lot boundaries and parcel areas, which is exactly what property developers need when evaluating land.

We've loaded three real parcels here — one in Sydney CBD, one in Parramatta, and one in Melbourne. When you click on a site, the panel on the right shows key assessment factors: zoning, FSR, height limit, bushfire status, heritage overlay, and flood risk.

These are the types of data our data engine will query from government APIs in real time — authoritative planning data that developers currently spend thousands of dollars and weeks of manual research to collect.

From here, the goal is one click to generate a full feasibility report.

### Slide 10 — Report Showcase (~50s)

And this is what the generated report looks like. What I want to highlight here is not the data itself — it's how the AI thinks.

The report follows a structured reasoning chain. Planning Analysis — what are you allowed to build here. Development Potential — what could this site actually become. Risk Assessment — what could go wrong. Financial Feasibility — does the money work. And finally, a Recommendation.

This is the blueprint we're building our AI engineering around. Each step feeds into the next, and the AI reaches a conclusion that's explainable and traceable — not a black box.

In this case, the AI recommendation is: Proceed — strong yield potential with manageable heritage constraint. A property developer can see exactly why the AI said yes, and what the one risk factor is.

### Slide 19 — Tech Stack (~40s)

Under the hood, LandIQ is built as a Turborepo monorepo with four purpose-built applications.

Three frontend apps — a Next.js marketing website with server-side rendering for SEO, an enterprise portal built with React and Vite as a single-page application, and an admin console also on React and Vite for platform management.

All three connect through a shared type system using Zod schemas to a unified REST API built on NestJS 11 with TypeORM. The API handles JWT authentication and multi-tenant role-based access control with five permission levels.

On the infrastructure side — PostgreSQL with PostGIS for geospatial queries and pgvector for AI embeddings, Redis with BullMQ for async job processing, and AWS S3 for file storage.

Everything deploys to AWS Sydney — EC2, S3, and CloudFront — with a full GitHub Actions CI/CD pipeline running lint, test, build, and deploy on every push.
